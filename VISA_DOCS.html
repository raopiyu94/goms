VISA related documentations

Documentation and discipline

Make sure to arrive at the biometrics and visa interview session well before time. It does not hurt to arrive early. Since there a lot of applicants, there may be a lot of traffic and you may arrive late then. Do not be nervous during the visa interview. Keep a calm mind and answer the questions asked by the interviewer honestly and confidently. Do not try to be dishonest with the interviewer. Try to keep all your documents in a harmonium file, arrange the files in decreasing order of importance i.e the way it is mentioned below.

Documents Required

  1. Passport (Valid)
  2. I-20
  3. Appointment letter
  4. Sevis fee receipt
  5. Admit letter of the University that you are going to attend
  6. Admit and reject letters of all other universities you applied to
  7. GRE and TOEFL Mark sheets
  8. Mark sheets and Transcripts of all the semesters of Bachelor’s Degree if you have completed your Bachelor’s Degree. If not, mark sheets and transcripts of all the semesters of which you have received the result. If you received a passing certificate during your first year, then keep that too
  9. Financial Documents include the financial assets you want to show that you have enough money to fund your education for Master’s. Preferably the amount shown should be more than amount shown in your I-20. If you have taken a loan, make sure to keep the loan sanction letter.
  10. If you are not the primary sponsor for your education, then you need to prepare an affidavit of support stating the name of the person who will sponsor your education along with their signatures on the affidavit. If you have secondary sponsor, you will also need to mention the name and have their signature on the affidavit of support.
  11. Keep Income tax returns (ITR) of your sponsors for the last 3 years. Make sure to keep this updated.
  12. For financial documents, you need to show assets like bank statements, fixed deposits, provident funds, chit funds. Public provident funds are also fine as long as there is a document from the bank stating the amount withdrawable at that point of time.
  13. Keep your property papers ready. Though it may not be asked during the interview, just keep it as a precaution.
  14. Mark sheets and passing certificate of 10th and 12th grade.
  15. Extra-curricular certificates.
